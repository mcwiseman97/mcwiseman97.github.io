Design Principles

Proximity - Kayla Roberts —

As you can see, there are three different groupings of words. They each have a larger heading, with a smaller sub-heading.
Also, the spacing between the four images on the top are the same and are very close. While the spacing between those
images and those images at the bottom of the picture is larger, showing that they are separate from each other.

Alignment - Lucas —

Etsy uses Alignment well as shown in the picture. All the images are perfectly aligned with equal spaces in every direction.

Repetition - Michael Wiseman —

Repetition can easily be seen in the main page of Etsy. The most visible form is with color. The website uses orange in many places,
and is used well with different shades of orange so it’s not plain. The use of different shades of orange doesn’t overwhelm the user
but provides a warm comforting feel.

Contrast - Avery Tutton—

This picture gives a great example of the contrast used on this site. Contrast can help the developer organize and better layout the page
for a great user experience, you can also manipulate contrast in many different ways. The contrast given in the example uses color to
catch the attention of the user and have items stand out, the developer also used different shadings of a same color to set a priority
of where your eyes should wander and in what order.

Typography - Avery Tutton—

You can see the organization in the different types of fonts, one type of text organizes the price, the other labels the title of each
item and the provider to better organize the site. The organization provided by the different types of styles will give the user a
better and more aesthetically pleasing experience. It is important though to catch the eye of the user and bring attention to the
more important information on the page without overwhelming the user.

Site Purpose Statement

The sites purpose is to pose the importance of design. The site represents what a great and working website should look like.
